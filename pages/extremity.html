Extremity and Amputation Injuries (F006)

General Principles

  • DO NOT allow fractures to distract from life-threatening injuries
  • Document peripheral neurovascular status before and after splinting
  • Document approximate time of injury
  • Consider pain management if stable

Fracture/Dislocation Management

If neurovascular compromise present:
  • Blanching skin
  • Absent pulses
  • Mottled appearance
  • Loss of sensation

Action: Attempt reduction to improve perfusion (2 attempts max), then splint

Approved dislocation reductions:
  • WITHOUT neurovascular compromise: Patella only
  • WITH neurovascular compromise (blanching skin): Ankles
  • Reduction of OPEN dislocations: NOT ALLOWED
Standard care:
  • Splint/apply traction as needed
  • Immobilize joints above and below injury

Bleeding Control

  • Direct pressure
  • Tourniquet
  • Hemostatic agent
  • Pressure point
  • For junctional injuries: Consider wound packing

Amputation Care

  • DO NOT delay transport for tissue retrieval
  • Rinse amputated part with sterile saline/water if grossly contaminated
  • Wrap part in saline-moistened gauze
  • Place tissue in plastic bag or container
  • Place bag/container on ice - AVOID freezing
  • For partial amputation: Splint part in anatomical position
