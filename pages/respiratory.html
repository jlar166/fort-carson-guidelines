Respiratory Emergencies (C001-C004)

Standard Dyspnea Care

Position of comfort, maintain airway, oxygen to maintain SpO2 90-98%, obtain VS, apply EtCO2, early 12-lead EKG if indicated.

Treatment by Lung Sounds

Wheezes/Rhonchi (Asthma/COPD):
  • Albuterol Nebulizer: 2.5mg in 3mL NS every 10 min (Adult/Peds >2)
  • Ipratropium: 0.5mg with albuterol (Adult), 0.25mg (Peds)
  • Dexamethasone: 10mg IV/IO/IM/PO (Adult), 0.5mg/kg (Peds, max 10mg)
  • Consider CPAP if no improvement
  • Magnesium Sulfate: 2g in 50mL NS over 10min (severe cases)
  • Epinephrine 1:1,000 IM: 0.3mg for severe attacks not responding
Rales (CHF/Pulmonary Edema):
  • Nitroglycerin: 0.4-1.2mg sublingual based on BP
  • Consider early CPAP
  • Albuterol: If EtCO2 reveals bronchospasm
Stridor (Croup/Obstruction):
  • For Croup: Epinephrine 1:1,000 nebulized - 5mg (Adult), 0.5mg/kg (Peds, max 5mg)
  • Dexamethasone: 10mg IV/IO/IM/PO (Adult), 0.5mg/kg (Peds)
  • For FBAO: Heimlich maneuver, visualize with laryngoscope, consider cricothyrotomy
