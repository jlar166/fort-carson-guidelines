    Medication Assisted Airway Management (MAAM)

    Description

    1. Guideline may only be utilized by Paramedics who have a current waiver by the medical director. MAAM can only be used for patients that are being treated on federal land or en route to/from a federal installation.
      • MAAM can be time consuming and is never an emergent airway. It is a complex airway management technique that requires substantial organization and understanding on the part of the paramedic.
    2. MAAM certified paramedics must maintain proficiency as established by local medical director.
    3. All medications in this guideline will be given by standing order.
      • Only MAAM waivered paramedics are allowed to administer MAAM specific medications.

    Indications (general)

    1. Age of the patient ‚â•13 years old
    2. MAAM candidates include, but not limited to;
      • Combative closed head injury patients, with or without trismus; or suspected subarachnoid hemorrhage, intracranial masses, or ischemic strokes resulting in significant impairment of mental status with resultant combativeness and/or trismus.
      • Glasgow Coma Scale ‚â§ 8
      • Status seizures
      • Severe respiratory failure and or impairment with an intact gag reflex or imminent respiratory arrest

    Selection Criteria

    1. Principles of good selection will result in avoidance of almost all of the common difficulties in failing to secure a tracheal intubation.
    2. There are three (3) basic rules regarding patient selection that must be reviewed every time a MAAM procedure is considered. These rules incorporate and summarize the general concepts of selection criteria outlined in detail in the MAAM course and are formatted as questions.
      • Can I get a good facial seal with the Bag-Valve-Mask?
      • Is the airway patent?
      • Do I think I can place an advanced airway in this patient?

    Contraindications

    1. Any absolute contraindication to the induction agent or paralytic
    2. Patients who DO NOT meet the selection criteria for intubation as discussed above
    3. Patients who DO NOT possess a gag reflex, rendering MAAM medications unnecessary
    4. Age of the patient <13 YEARS OLD

    Complications

    1. Cardiac dysrhythmias related to the use of succinylcholine
    2. Malignant Hyperthermia or the suspected presence of pseudocholinesterase deficiency
    3. Failure to Intubate
    4. Unrecognized esophageal intubation
    5. Vomiting and aspiration
    6. Hypotension
    7. Excessive gagging on back-up airway when used in failed intubations

    MAAM Algorithm

    1. Can I get a good facial seal with the Bag-Valve-Mask?
    2. Is the airway patent?
    3. Do I think I can place and advanced airway?
    4. Are there valid indications?
    5. Is the patient ‚â• 13 years old

    Attach all monitoring devices (EtCO2/ EKG/SpO2/ NIBP)
    Preoxygenate 3 to 5 minutes
    Discuss airway back up plan
    Establish Vascular Access
    • Normotensive: Administer 250 to 500 mL NS IV/IO
    • Hypoperfusion: Follow FLUID THERAPY GUIDELINE
    If inadequate perfusion initiate EPINEPHRINE INFUSION
    Equipment set up
    • Video Laryngoscopy (Preferred) or blade/handle
    • Supraglottic Airway
    • Bougie/stylet
    • Suction
    • ET Tube with 10 mL syringe
    • Securing device
    • Cricothyrotomy kit
    ***MAAM Waivered Paramedic ONLY***
    Step 1: Induce
    • KETAMINE: 200 mg IV/IO
      • or
    • MIDAZOLAM: 5 mg IV/IO
    Step 2: Paralyze
    • ROCURONIUM: 100 mg IV/IO
      • or
    • SUCCINYLCHOLINE: 200 mg IV/IO
    Step 3: Insert Advanced Airway:
    • See AIRWAY MANAGEMENT GUIDELINE
    • Ensure a NC is placed at high flow during attempts
    Step 4: Confirm advanced airway placement (4)
    • Positive waveform capnography (print)
    • Bilateral breath sounds
    • Negative epigastric sounds
    • One additional method
    Step 5: Secure advanced airway
    • Place OG Tube (if allowed AND time allows)
    Step 6: Sedation: PRN every 10 minutes
    • KETAMINE: 100 mg IV/IO
      • OR
    • MIDAZOLAM: Up to 5 mg IV/IO
      • AND/or
    • FENTANYL: 50-100 mcg IV/IO
    NO
    Monitor EtCO2/SpO2 and manage the airway utilizing the AIRWAY MANAGEMENT GUIDELINE as best as possible
    Special Considerations
    • When applicable, discuss procedure with patient and/or family members.
      • Remember to check if there is a valid DNR in place.
    • Succinylcholine is ONLY to be given once.
    • It is anticipated that virtually no patient will ever require surgical cricothyrotomy as an emergent alternate airway.
      • It should ONLY be utilized if the patient continues to deteriorate due to an inadequate airway.
    • VECURONIUM can be administered at 10 mg IV/IO to maintain paralysis for long distance transports.
    • Documentation is critical, describe a detailed account of the event.
    • Procedure MUST be followed by sedation
    • Watch for post procedure hypoperfusion and be ready for resuscitation.
