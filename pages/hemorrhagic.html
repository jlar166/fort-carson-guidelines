    Hemorrhagic Shock (F002)

    Signs and Symptoms of Poor Perfusion

    • Delayed capillary refill >2 seconds
    • Adult: Systolic BP <90< /li>
    • Pediatric: Low systolic BP for age (see Pediatric Field Guide)
    • Depressed or deteriorating mental status
    • Absent or decreased peripheral pulses

    Treatment Protocol

    Control Bleeding:
    • Direct pressure
    • Tourniquet
    • Hemostatic agent
    • Pressure point
    • Pelvic binder
    • Wound packing (junctional injuries)
    Manage Heat Loss:
    • Apply space blanket
    • Keep patient warm
    Fluid Therapy (Permissive Hypotension):
    • Goal: Maintain BP 80-100 systolic (or >Peds Guide recommendation)
    • Dosing: 10mL/kg NS IV/IO, repeat until:
      • Restoration of distal pulses AND/OR
      • Improved mental status AND/OR
      • Permissive hypotension achieved (80-100 systolic)
    Tranexamic Acid (TXA):
    • Criteria: Suspected shock <3 hours AND NO contraindications
    • Contraindications:
      • Age <13 years
      • Isolated head injury
      • Time of injury >3 hours
      • Active intravascular clotting (DVT/PE)
      • GI bleed
    • Dosing:
      • Adult: Mix 1g TXA in 50mL NS, infuse over 10min IV/IO
      • Peds <13: NOT ALLOWED
    "Triad of Death" - Watch For:
    • Hypothermia
    • Coagulopathy
    • Acidosis
