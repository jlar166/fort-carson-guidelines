    Airway Management (B002)

    Definition

    The goal of airway management is to ensure patients receive adequate ventilation and oxygenation. This can be accomplished through various techniques including basic positioning, adjuncts, and advanced level airways.

    • Assessment is crucial to success helping to identify complications before they arise.
    • A difficult airway is in which a conventionally trained provider experiences difficulty with maintaining adequate ventilation and oxygenation.
    • Successful airway management is defined as placement of an ETT OR the ability to effectively oxygenate/ventilate.

    Description

    1. Guideline modified to achieve highest EMS provider safety during infectious disease crisis (e.g., COVID-19).
    2. All airway management performed with EMS provider in recommended PPE.
    3. All attempts should be made to optimize oxygenation/ventilation prior to advanced airway placement.
    4. Advanced airway placement in conjunction with continuous waveform EtCO2 and pulse ox.
    5. Ensure perfusion status prior to advanced airway (BP > 90, EtCO2 > 30, MAP > 60).
      • Administer early FLUID THERAPY
      • Consider EPINEPHRINE INFUSION for inadequate perfusion
    6. NO MORE THAN (3) ETT TOTAL ATTEMPTS PER PATIENT
    7. Oxygen administration at 8 L via nasal cannula during ETT attempt.
    8. Consider cricothyrotomy where effective oxygenation/ventilation cannot be provided.
