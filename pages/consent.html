    Consent (A003)

    Decision Making Capacity

    A person has appropriate decision making capacity if they:

    • Are alert, oriented, reliable, calm, and cooperative
    • Understand the nature and consequences of their illness or injury
    • Understand the nature and consequences of proposed or refused treatment
    • Have NO IMPAIRED JUDGEMENT

    Age of Consent

    • Patient must be over 18 years of age OR
    • Between 15 and 18 years and "emancipated"

    Implied Consent

    Consent is "implied" when the patient is unable to consent to treatment due to age, mental status, or medical condition AND no responsible party is available to grant that consent.

    Legal Considerations: In no event should legal consent procedures be allowed to delay immediately required treatment.
