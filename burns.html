Burn Injuries (F007)

General Burn Care

  • Stop the burning process
  • Remove constrictive clothing or jewelry
  • Monitor airway closely for inhalation burns
  • Cover with dry dressing
  • Consider carbon monoxide/cyanide poisoning if in confined space
  • Use tepid water to cool burns for 20-30min (avoid hypothermia)

Burn Percentage Requiring Fluid Therapy

  • Adult: 2nd degree burn >15% BSA
  • Pediatric or Elderly: 2nd degree burn >10% BSA
  • All Ages: 3rd degree burn >2%
Fluid Therapy Rates:
  • Age >14: 500 mL/hr NS or LR
  • Age 5-13: 250 mL/hr NS or LR
  • Age <5: 125 mL/hr NS or LR

Burn Types

Thermal Burns:
  • Cool with tepid water
  • Cover with dry dressing
  • Fluid therapy as indicated
Chemical Burns:
  • Consult Emergency Response Guide FIRST
  • Brush off any dry chemicals/powder
  • Continuously flush with water if appropriate
  • For eye involvement: Consider topical ophthalmic anesthetic
  • Use appropriate PPE
Electrical Burns:
  • Suspect spinal injury (tetanic muscle contractions)
  • Cardiac monitor and 12-lead EKG required
  • Cover with dry dressing
  • Fluid therapy as indicated
BSA Assessment: Patient's palm = 1% of their BSA. Use "Rule of 9's" as reference.
