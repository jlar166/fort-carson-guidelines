Introduction

The following guidelines have been developed and approved by the FCFD and EACH EMS in conjunction with a working group representing FCFD, EACH EMS, AMR, CSFD, SCRETAC, SECRETAC and P2PRETAC.

Purpose: These guidelines delineate the expected standard of care and acts allowed for EMT, EMT-IV and paramedic providers under local medical direction.

Key Principles

  • Guidelines are evidence-based where possible
  • Reviewed on a six-month basis
  • No substitute for sound clinical judgment
  • Contact medical control when situations arise outside guidelines

Guideline Format

The electronic format includes hyperlinks for quick navigation between related guidelines, procedures, and medications.

Patient Determination (A002)

Description

When in doubt as to whether an individual is a "patient", err on the side of caution and perform a full assessment as well as documentation.

Criteria for Patient Status

  • Person has a complaint resulting in a call for help
  • Illness or injury suspected based on appearance or MOI
  • Person meets decision-making capacity criteria
Important: If further care and evaluation is warranted AND the patient is refusing to be treated/transported, then a refusal MUST be completed.

Consent (A003)

Decision Making Capacity

A person has appropriate decision making capacity if they:

  • Are alert, oriented, reliable, calm, and cooperative
  • Understand the nature and consequences of their illness or injury
  • Understand the nature and consequences of proposed or refused treatment
  • Have NO IMPAIRED JUDGEMENT

Age of Consent

  • Patient must be over 18 years of age OR
  • Between 15 and 18 years and "emancipated"

Implied Consent

Consent is "implied" when the patient is unable to consent to treatment due to age, mental status, or medical condition AND no responsible party is available to grant that consent.

Legal Considerations: In no event should legal consent procedures be allowed to delay immediately required treatment.

Pain Management (B004)

Objectives

The objective of pain management is not complete relief of all pain, but rather to make a patient's pain tolerable to allow for assessment, treatment, and transport.

Medication Options

Mild to Moderate Pain:
  • Acetaminophen IV/IO:
    • Adult <50kg: 500mg
    • Adult >50kg: 1,000mg
    • Pediatric: 15mg/kg (MAX 1000mg)
Moderate to Severe Pain:
  • Ketamine:
    • Adult: 30mg IV/IO or 50mg IM/IN (up to 3 doses)
    • Pediatric >7: 0.3mg/kg IV/IO or 0.5mg/kg IM/IN
  • Fentanyl:
    • Adult: 50-100mcg IV/IO or IN/IM
    • Pediatric: 1mcg/kg IV/IO or IN/IM
  • Hydromorphone:
    • Adult: 0.5mg IV/IO or IM (NOT REPEATED)
    • Pediatric: NOT allowed
Precautions:
  • Monitor ECG, SpO2, EtCO2, and BP continuously
  • Consider Â½ typical dosing in elderly patients
  • Watch for respiratory depression
  • Pain medications should only be given to hemodynamically stable patients

Respiratory Emergencies (C001-C004)

Standard Dyspnea Care

Position of comfort, maintain airway, oxygen to maintain SpO2 90-98%, obtain VS, apply EtCO2, early 12-lead EKG if indicated.

Treatment by Lung Sounds

Wheezes/Rhonchi (Asthma/COPD):
  • Albuterol Nebulizer: 2.5mg in 3mL NS every 10 min (Adult/Peds >2)
  • Ipratropium: 0.5mg with albuterol (Adult), 0.25mg (Peds)
  • Dexamethasone: 10mg IV/IO/IM/PO (Adult), 0.5mg/kg (Peds, max 10mg)
  • Consider CPAP if no improvement
  • Magnesium Sulfate: 2g in 50mL NS over 10min (severe cases)
  • Epinephrine 1:1,000 IM: 0.3mg for severe attacks not responding
Rales (CHF/Pulmonary Edema):
  • Nitroglycerin: 0.4-1.2mg sublingual based on BP
  • Consider early CPAP
  • Albuterol: If EtCO2 reveals bronchospasm
Stridor (Croup/Obstruction):
  • For Croup: Epinephrine 1:1,000 nebulized - 5mg (Adult), 0.5mg/kg (Peds, max 5mg)
  • Dexamethasone: 10mg IV/IO/IM/PO (Adult), 0.5mg/kg (Peds)
  • For FBAO: Heimlich maneuver, visualize with laryngoscope, consider cricothyrotomy

Cardiac Emergencies (D001-D008)

Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS)

Cardiac Alert Criteria:
  • ACS symptoms AND
  • 12-lead showing:
    • ST elevation â‰¥1mm in 2+ contiguous leads, OR
    • ST elevation â‰¥1mm in aVR with widespread depression, OR
    • 2mm ST depression in V1-V2 (posterior), OR
    • Computer interpretation of "ACUTE MI"
Treatment:
  • Aspirin: 162-324mg PO (Adult only)
  • Establish vascular access
  • If signs of poor perfusion: Fluid therapy 250-500mL NS
  • Consider pain management
  • Notify receiving hospital ASAP - request CARDIAC ALERT

Bradydysrhythmia

If Symptomatic with signs of poor perfusion:
  • Atropine:
    • Adult: 0.5mg IV/IO every 3min (MAX 3mg)
    • Peds: 0.02mg/kg IV/IO (MIN 0.1mg, MAX single dose 0.5mg)
  • Transcutaneous Pacing:
    • Adult: 60 bpm, increase energy until capture
    • Peds: 80 bpm, increase energy until capture
  • Epinephrine Infusion: 1mg in 1,000mL NS wide open to gravity

Tachydysrhythmia

Narrow Complex (QRS <0.12):
  • Vagal Maneuvers (if stable)
  • Adenosine:
    • Adult: 6-12mg rapid IV/IO with 20mL NS flush
    • Peds: 0.1mg/kg (max 12mg), repeat at 0.2mg/kg
  • Diltiazem (irregular):
    • Adult: 20mg IV/IO over 2-5min, repeat at 25mg after 15min
Wide Complex (QRS >0.12):
  • Amiodarone (regular monomorphic):
    • Adult: 150mg IV/IO over 10min
  • Magnesium (polymorphic/torsades):
    • Adult: 2g slow IV/IO over 10min
If Signs of Poor Perfusion:
  • Synchronized Cardioversion
  • Consider sedation with Midazolam first if stable

Medical Cardiac Arrest

Adult >19 Years:
  • Withhold Resuscitation if ALL present:
    • Unwitnessed arrest AND
    • Age â‰¥73 years AND
    • PEA/Asystole (non-shockable)
  • Chest Compressions: 2 minutes cycles
  • Airway: First 3 cycles passive O2, then PPV
  • For VF/VT: Defibrillate at MAX joules
  • Consider TOR after: 4 cycles (8min) AND EtCO2 <10
Pediatric <18 Years:
  • Epinephrine 1:10,000:
    • >13: 1mg IV/IO (MAX 3 doses)
    • <13: 0.01mg/kg IV/IO
  • Amiodarone:
    • >13: 300mg IV/IO, then 150mg
    • <13: 5mg/kg IV/IO (up to 3 doses)
  • Defibrillation:
    • >13: MAX joules
    • <13: 4 J/kg
  • Consider TOR after: 10 cycles (20min)

Cerebral Vascular Accident (E002)

BE-FAST Stroke Screening

  • Balance - sudden loss of balance or coordination
  • Eyes - sudden change in vision or abnormal pupils
  • Face - does one side droop?
  • Arms - does one arm drift?
  • Speech - is speech slurred or strange?
  • Time - when was last known normal?

Stroke Alert Activation & Transport

Last Known Normal <4 hours:
  • Transport to closest facility that provides TPA
  • Facilities: St. Francis, Memorial North, Memorial Central, Penrose Main
Last Known Normal 4-24 hours:
  • Transport to facility with intracranial intervention
  • Facilities: Memorial Central, Penrose Main
Last Known Normal >24 hours:
  • Transport to closest appropriate facility
Special Considerations:
  • Elevate head of bed 30Â° if possible
  • Establish two 18G or larger IV sites if possible
  • Rule out hypoglycemia, seizure, infection, hyperthermia
  • Monitor BP but avoid excessive fluid administration

Seizures (E003)

Medical/Trauma Etiology

First-Line Treatment:
  • Midazolam:
    • Adult: 5mg IV/IO/IM or 5-10mg IN (MAX 3 doses)
    • Peds: 0.1mg/kg IV/IO/IM/IN (max single dose 5mg)
  • OR Ativan (Lorazepam):
    • Adult: 2mg IV/IO/deep IM (MAX 3 doses)
    • Peds: 0.1mg/kg IV/IO/deep IM (max single dose 4mg)
If seizure uncontrolled AND imminent respiratory compromise:
  • Consider MAAM (see MAAM guideline)
  • Use benzodiazepines for sedation post-MAAM

Eclampsia

  • Magnesium Sulfate:
    • Adult: 5g in 50mL NS/D5W over 5-10min (NOT repeated)
  • Patient does NOT need to be actively seizing
  • Consider up to 6 weeks post-partum
Important: Assess for occult trauma and substance abuse. In patients >50 years, seizures may be due to dysrhythmias or stroke.

Diabetic Emergency (E007)

Hypoglycemia (<60 mg/dL)

If able to follow commands and maintain airway:
  • Oral Glucose: 1-2 full tubes (15-30g) buccal every 10min
If unable to maintain airway OR no improvement:
  • Dextrose IV/IO:
    • Adult: 12.5-25g (50mL of 50% solution)
    • Peds <13: 10mL/kg of 10% solution slowly over 10min
    • Repeat after 10min if needed until BGL >60
If vascular access unsuccessful:
  • Glucagon IM:
    • Adult: 1mg IM (NOT repeated)
    • Peds: 0.5mg IM (NOT repeated)

Hyperglycemia

  • Establish vascular access
  • Fluid Therapy: 20mL/kg NS IV/IO until improved perfusion
  • Cardiac monitor and 12-lead EKG
Special Considerations:
  • DO NOT let alcohol confuse the clinical picture - alcoholics frequently develop hypoglycemia
  • If symptoms don't resolve after treatment, consider other causes
  • Patient must eat complex carbohydrates before refusing care

Allergy/Anaphylaxis (E005)

Moderate to Severe Allergic Reaction

First-Line Treatment:
  • Epinephrine 1:1,000 IM:
    • Adult: 0.3mg (0.3mL) IM every 3-5min
    • Peds: 0.01mg/kg (0.01mL/kg) IM every 3-5min
  • Dexamethasone:
    • Adult: 10mg IV/IO/IM/PO
    • Peds: 0.5mg/kg IV/IO/IM/PO (max 10mg)
  • Diphenhydramine (Benadryl):
    • Adult: 50mg IV/IO/IM/PO
    • Peds: 1-2mg/kg IV/IO/IM/PO (max 50mg)
If Dyspnea present:
  • Albuterol Nebulizer:
    • Adult/Peds >2: 2.5mg in 3mL NS every 10min
    • Peds <2: 1.25mg in 3mL NS
  • Consider CPAP if unresolved
If Signs of Poor Perfusion:
  • Epinephrine Infusion: 1mg in 1,000mL NS wide open to gravity
    • Titrate to BP >90 systolic, return of distal pulses, improved mental status

Generalized Allergic Reaction (Mild)

  • Diphenhydramine: Same dosing as above
  • General supportive care

Standard Trauma Care (F001)

General Principles

  • Scene times should be 10 minutes or less if possible
  • Invasive treatments should be performed en route
  • Early notification to receiving facility
  • EtCO2 SHOULD be utilized in any major trauma
  • Avoid hypothermia - maintain patient warmth

Initial Assessment

  • Consider C-spine precautions (see Spinal Motion Restriction)
  • Maintain patent airway and OXYGENATION
  • Monitor EtCO2 and obtain VS
  • Complete neurologic exam
  • Control bleeding:
    • Direct pressure and/or
    • Tourniquet and/or
    • Hemostatic agent and/or
    • Pressure point and/or
    • Pelvic binder and/or
    • Wound packing (junctional injuries ONLY)
  • Establish vascular access if clinically indicated
  • Treat for shock if clinically indicated
Pregnant Trauma Patients:
  • Avoid supine position - left lateral recumbent >30Â°
  • VS may be altered (increased HR, decreased BP, increased blood volume)
  • Traumatic arrest with gestation >23 weeks = rapid transport for perimortem c-section

Hemorrhagic Shock (F002)

Signs and Symptoms of Poor Perfusion

  • Delayed capillary refill >2 seconds
  • Adult: Systolic BP <90
  • Pediatric: Low systolic BP for age (see Pediatric Field Guide)
  • Depressed or deteriorating mental status
  • Absent or decreased peripheral pulses

Treatment Protocol

Control Bleeding:
  • Direct pressure
  • Tourniquet
  • Hemostatic agent
  • Pressure point
  • Pelvic binder
  • Wound packing (junctional injuries)
Manage Heat Loss:
  • Apply space blanket
  • Keep patient warm
Fluid Therapy (Permissive Hypotension):
  • Goal: Maintain BP 80-100 systolic (or >Peds Guide recommendation)
  • Dosing: 10mL/kg NS IV/IO, repeat until:
    • Restoration of distal pulses AND/OR
    • Improved mental status AND/OR
    • Permissive hypotension achieved (80-100 systolic)
Tranexamic Acid (TXA):
  • Criteria: Suspected shock <3 hours AND NO contraindications
  • Contraindications:
    • Age <13 years
    • Isolated head injury
    • Time of injury >3 hours
    • Active intravascular clotting (DVT/PE)
    • GI bleed
  • Dosing:
    • Adult: Mix 1g TXA in 50mL NS, infuse over 10min IV/IO
    • Peds <13: NOT ALLOWED
"Triad of Death" - Watch For:
  • Hypothermia
  • Coagulopathy
  • Acidosis

Burn Injuries (F007)

General Burn Care

  • Stop the burning process
  • Remove constrictive clothing or jewelry
  • Monitor airway closely for inhalation burns
  • Cover with dry dressing
  • Consider carbon monoxide/cyanide poisoning if in confined space
  • Use tepid water to cool burns for 20-30min (avoid hypothermia)

Burn Percentage Requiring Fluid Therapy

  • Adult: 2nd degree burn >15% BSA
  • Pediatric or Elderly: 2nd degree burn >10% BSA
  • All Ages: 3rd degree burn >2%
Fluid Therapy Rates:
  • Age >14: 500 mL/hr NS or LR
  • Age 5-13: 250 mL/hr NS or LR
  • Age <5: 125 mL/hr NS or LR

Burn Types

Thermal Burns:
  • Cool with tepid water
  • Cover with dry dressing
  • Fluid therapy as indicated
Chemical Burns:
  • Consult Emergency Response Guide FIRST
  • Brush off any dry chemicals/powder
  • Continuously flush with water if appropriate
  • For eye involvement: Consider topical ophthalmic anesthetic
  • Use appropriate PPE
Electrical Burns:
  • Suspect spinal injury (tetanic muscle contractions)
  • Cardiac monitor and 12-lead EKG required
  • Cover with dry dressing
  • Fluid therapy as indicated
BSA Assessment: Patient's palm = 1% of their BSA. Use "Rule of 9's" as reference.

Extremity and Amputation Injuries (F006)

General Principles

  • DO NOT allow fractures to distract from life-threatening injuries
  • Document peripheral neurovascular status before and after splinting
  • Document approximate time of injury
  • Consider pain management if stable

Fracture/Dislocation Management

If neurovascular compromise present:
  • Blanching skin
  • Absent pulses
  • Mottled appearance
  • Loss of sensation

Action: Attempt reduction to improve perfusion (2 attempts max), then splint

Approved dislocation reductions:
  • WITHOUT neurovascular compromise: Patella only
  • WITH neurovascular compromise (blanching skin): Ankles
  • Reduction of OPEN dislocations: NOT ALLOWED
Standard care:
  • Splint/apply traction as needed
  • Immobilize joints above and below injury

Bleeding Control

  • Direct pressure
  • Tourniquet
  • Hemostatic agent
  • Pressure point
  • For junctional injuries: Consider wound packing

Amputation Care

  • DO NOT delay transport for tissue retrieval
  • Rinse amputated part with sterile saline/water if grossly contaminated
  • Wrap part in saline-moistened gauze
  • Place tissue in plastic bag or container
  • Place bag/container on ice - AVOID freezing
  • For partial amputation: Splint part in anatomical position

Medication Overview (H000)

Principles of Administration

  • Closed loop communication
  • Cross-check with another provider when possible
  • Verify appropriateness including routes
  • Use Pediatric Field Guide for pediatric dosing

Medication Errors

The system follows a "Just Culture" approach - self-reporting is encouraged and focuses on system improvements rather than punitive measures.

Dose Modification: Providers may lower recommended doses if justified and documented. Reasons include liver/kidney failure, age, weight, and potential drug interactions.

Category X Fetal abnormalities demonstrated; risks clearly outweigh benefits
